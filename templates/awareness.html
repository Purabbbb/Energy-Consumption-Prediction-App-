Common myths about electricity usage

These short myth-vs-fact pairs are handy talking points when explaining energy awareness to non-technical audiences.

Fact: For LEDs and CFLs, it is almost always better to switch them off when not needed. The tiny inrush current is negligible compared to leaving them on for minutes or hours.

Fact: Individually small, but together with TV boxes, speakers and routers, standby loads can add up to 5‚Äì10% of your bill. Smart strips or scheduled turn-off can make a visible difference.

Fact: Most savings come from timing, insulation, and smarter controls ‚Äì not from ‚Äúsuffering in the dark‚Äù. The goal is same comfort, less waste.
